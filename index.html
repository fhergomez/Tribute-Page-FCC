Octavio Paz

1914 - 1998

“La Libertad no necesita alas
lo que necesita es echar raices”
Octavio Paz

He is considered by many as one of the most influential writers of the 20th century and one of the greatest poets of all time.

Octavio Paz Life Events & Accomplishments:

  • Born March 31, 1914 in México City, México
  • in 1931, Paz published his first poems, including "Cabellera".
  • In 1937, Paz was invited to the Second International Writers Congress in Defense of Culture in Spain during the country's civil war
  • In 1937 he married Elena Garro, who is considered one of Mexico's finest writers.
  • In 1943, Paz received a Guggenheim fellowship and used it to study at the University of California at Berkeley in the United States.
  • In 1945, he was sent to Paris, where he wrote El Laberinto de la Soledad ("The Labyrinth of Solitude").
  • In 1954, he returned to México City where he wrote his great poem "Piedra de sol" ("Sunstone").
  • In 1962 he was named Mexico's ambassador to India.
  • In 1965, he married Marie-José Tramini, a French woman who would be his wife for the rest of his life.
  • In October 1968, he resigned from the diplomatic service in protest of the Mexican government's massacre of student demonstrators in the Plaza de las Tres Culturas in Tlatelolco.
  • From 1969 to 1970 he was Simon Bolivar Chair Professor at Cambridge University.
  • In 1977, he won the 1977 Jerusalem Prize for literature on the theme of individual freedom.
  • In 1982, he won the Neustadt Prize.
  • In 1990, he was awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature.
  • On April 19, 1998, he died of cancer in Mexico City
